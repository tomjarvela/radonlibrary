About

Tom Jarvela is a resident of Stratham, NH where he lives with his wife and young children. A farmer by day he dabbles in tech wizardry by night (after the kids have gone to bed).

In the fall of 2021 his family discovered that that they had ridiculously high levels of radon in their home. Having since had their home mitigated, Tom has become a staunch radon awareness advocate with the express goal of helping as many New Hampshire residents test their homes as possible.

What is radon?

Radon is a naturally occurring, radioactive gas that can't been seen or smelled.

The only way to know the level of radon in your home is through testing.

Exposure to radon can cause lung cancer. 1

30% of N.H. Homes have elevated levels of radon. 2


1. https://www.epa.gov/radiation/what-radon-gas-it-dangerous
2. https://www.nh.gov/epht/environmental-topics/documents/radon.pdf

Test your home

Testing is the only way to know the level of radon in your home. New Hampshire residents now have two options for testing their homes at no cost:

Free Radon Test Kit

The New Hampshire Department of Health and Human Services is currently giving away free radon test kits to all NH residents.

Get your test kit from NH DHHS


Borrow A Radon Detector

Do you live in Brentwood, Derry, Sandown, or Stratham? If so, your library has an electronic radon detector available for you to borrow. Reserve it today!

If your library is not listed above, consider contacting them to recommend that they add one.
