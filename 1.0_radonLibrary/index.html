MISSION:

RADONLIBRARY was founded to educate New Hampshire residents about the importance of testing the level of radon in their homes.

We are working collaboratively with local communities to make sure that an electronic radon detector is available in every library in New Hampshire.

ABOUT TOM:

Tom Jarvela is a resident of Stratham, NH where he lives with his wife and young children.

A farmer by day he dabbles in  tech wizardry by night.

In the fall of 2021 his family discovered that that they had ridiculously high levels of radon in their home. Having since had their home mitigated, Tom has become a staunch radon awareness advocate with the express goal of helping as many New Hampshire residents test their homes as possible.

Mitigation

Higher than 4.0 pCi/L?

...then it is recommended to contact a certified radon mitigation contractor. They will be able to assess if a mitigation system should be considered.

Radon mitigation systems, simply put, work like a vaccuum. A fan draws radon out from under the home before it has a chance to enter the home and safely vent it outside.

When my family discovered that the level of radon in our home was 50x the EPA's recommended level we contacted Advanced Radon Mitigation & Water Treatment. They were courteous, professional, and committed to helping my family breath easier - both figuratively and literally.

Get in touch with them to schedule your FREE on-site evaluation.

Radon Test Results

The EPA has set a radon action level of 4.0 pCi/L. It is recommended that any homes with concentrations higher than 4.0 pCi/L contact a certified radon mitigation contractor to assess the situation and make recommendations on remediation.

Radon Measurements:

4+ pCi/L - Ventilate, contact a certified radon mitigation contractor, and continue monitoring
2 - 4 pCi/L - Ventilate and monitor
0 - 2 pCi/L - Monitor
